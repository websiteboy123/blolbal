Introduction to Climate Change

Climate change is caused by the enhanced greenhouse effect. Greenhouse effect are indeed useful in our planet Earth. They reflect 40% of the heat coming from the sun. However, there are bad sides of greenhouse effect. That is called enhanced greenhouse effect. They cause bad air pollution, ice melts, and ocean level rises. Also, the air is getting warmer because of climate change. Examples of enhanced greenhouse effect are burning fossil fuels

How to prevent Climate Change?

Conserve Energy- --> Minimize the amount of energy you use for heating and cooling, convert to energy-saving electric appliances. Up to 900 kg of CO2e can be removed from your home's carbon footprint annually by increasing its energy efficiency. --> Turning off the lights when not really needed and using sunlight as an alternative, unplugging appliances when they are not in use, and walking instead of driving are all cases of preserving the energy that helps in reducing the demand on the earth's natural resources.

-Take Care of Your Surroundings- --> Help in cleaning your environment. One of the ways for this is to dispose your trash properly. Land and water contamination caused by poorly disposed trash truly affects humans, animals, plants and as well as the environment that may leads in contribution to the causes of climate change. --> If you have yourself with some free time, try participating in local cleanup activities in your own community that aims to safeguard green spaces, waterways, and other natural areas.

-Reject, Reduce, Re-use, Repair, Recycle- --> Following these 5R concept can help to conserve natural resources, reduce pollution, lower greenhouse gas emissions, save energy, and reduce trash to landfills. These methods help to make the environment healthier and more sustainable by reducing the environmental impact of the increasing waste production.
